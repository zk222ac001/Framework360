# NIS2 Readiness Content

This document contains starter content for positioning Framework360 as a NIS2 readiness product for SMEs and compliance consultants.

## Disclaimer

Framework360 supports compliance readiness work. It does not provide legal advice and does not guarantee compliance or certification.

## Readiness Domains

### 1. Governance and Accountability

Requirement: The organization has assigned management responsibility for cybersecurity and NIS2 readiness.

Implementation guidance:
- Assign a responsible manager or security owner.
- Document responsibilities.
- Review cybersecurity status regularly with management.

Example evidence:
- Management meeting minutes
- Security responsibility matrix
- Cybersecurity governance policy

Risk if missing:
- No clear ownership of cybersecurity risks and regulatory obligations.

Suggested task:
- Assign a cybersecurity owner and document responsibilities.

### 2. Risk Management

Requirement: The organization performs regular cybersecurity risk assessments.

Implementation guidance:
- Identify critical systems, vendors, and business processes.
- Assess threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, and impact.
- Review risks at least annually or after major changes.

Example evidence:
- Risk register
- Risk assessment report
- Management approval of risk treatment plan

Risk if missing:
- Security decisions may be reactive and undocumented.

Suggested task:
- Create a cybersecurity risk register for critical systems and vendors.

### 3. Incident Handling

Requirement: The organization has a documented incident response process.

Implementation guidance:
- Define incident categories and severity levels.
- Define escalation contacts and responsibilities.
- Document reporting procedures and communication flow.

Example evidence:
- Incident response plan
- Incident log
- Tabletop exercise notes

Risk if missing:
- Incidents may be handled inconsistently and reporting deadlines may be missed.

Suggested task:
- Create and approve an incident response plan.

### 4. Business Continuity

Requirement: The organization has continuity and recovery procedures for critical services.

Implementation guidance:
- Identify critical business processes.
- Define recovery time objectives and recovery point objectives.
- Test backup and recovery procedures.

Example evidence:
- Business continuity plan
- Backup restore test
- Disaster recovery procedure

Risk if missing:
- Service disruption may cause financial, operational, or regulatory impact.

Suggested task:
- Document backup and recovery procedures for critical systems.

### 5. Supply Chain Security

Requirement: The organization manages cybersecurity risks from suppliers and service providers.

Implementation guidance:
- Identify critical suppliers.
- Review supplier security measures.
- Maintain contracts, DPAs, and SLAs where relevant.

Example evidence:
- Supplier list
- Vendor risk assessment
- Data processing agreement
- Service level agreement

Risk if missing:
- Supplier weaknesses may expose the organization to operational or data security risks.

Suggested task:
- Review all critical suppliers and document security expectations.

### 6. Access Control

Requirement: Access to systems and data is controlled according to business need.

Implementation guidance:
- Use unique user accounts.
- Apply least privilege.
- Review access regularly.
- Enable MFA for critical systems.

Example evidence:
- Access control policy
- User access review
- MFA configuration screenshot

Risk if missing:
- Unauthorized access may go undetected or remain active too long.

Suggested task:
- Perform access review for all critical systems.

### 7. Vulnerability Management

Requirement: The organization identifies and remediates technical vulnerabilities.

Implementation guidance:
- Track software and systems.
- Apply security updates.
- Review vulnerability scan results if available.

Example evidence:
- Patch management process
- Vulnerability scan report
- Update logs

Risk if missing:
- Known vulnerabilities may be exploited by attackers.

Suggested task:
- Define a patch and vulnerability management process.

### 8. Awareness and Training

Requirement: Employees receive relevant cybersecurity awareness training.

Implementation guidance:
- Provide training for all employees.
- Include phishing, password hygiene, data handling, and incident reporting.
- Track participation.

Example evidence:
- Training material
- Attendance records
- Awareness campaign records

Risk if missing:
- Employees may not recognize or report security threats.

Suggested task:
- Run annual cybersecurity awareness training.
